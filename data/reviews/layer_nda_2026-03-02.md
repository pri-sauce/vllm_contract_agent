**Risk Level:** HIGH  
**Clause Type:** general

**Issues Found:**

**Issue 1:** Lack of mutual obligations — This clause does not explicitly state that both parties are bound by its terms.
> **Evidence:** *'Senior Software Engineer, and the Employee has agreed to serve the Company"'*

**Issue 2:** No exceptions for publicly available information
> **Evidence:** *'None'*

By making these changes, we can improve the overall fairness and clarity of this contract provision.

### 🔴 8 DISPUTE RESOLUTION & GOVERNING LAW

**Risk Level:** HIGH  
**Clause Type:** dispute_resolution

**Issues Found:**

**Issue 1:** Mandatory arbitration without opt-out for injunctive relief — If signed as-is, this clause may limit the ability to seek injunctive relief in court, potentially harming the company's interests.
> **Evidence:** *'The arbitration shall be conducted by a sole arbitrator appointed by mutual agreement or, failing such agreement, by the Karnataka State Arbitration Centre."'*

**Issue 2:** Jurisdiction with which we have no presence — This clause may expose the company to litigation in a jurisdiction where it has no physical presence or connections, increasing risk and costs.
> **Evidence:** *'The courts in Bangalore shall have exclusive jurisdiction over any matters not subject to arbitration."'*

**Issue 3:** Arbitration conducted by a sole arbitrator without opt-out for injunctive relief — This clause may limit the ability to appeal or challenge the arbitrator's decisions, potentially leading to unfair outcomes.

**
> **Evidence:** *'The arbitration shall be conducted by a sole arbitrator appointed by mutual agreement or, failing such agreement, by the Karnataka State Arbitration Centre."'*

**Precise Redlines:**

**Redline 1:**
~~by the Karnataka State Arbitration Centre~~
→ `by a panel of three arbitrators with expertise in intellectual property and data breach claims`

**Analysis:**
**
The dispute resolution clause contains several issues that require attention. The mandatory arbitration without opt-out for injunctive relief clause (ISSUE 1) may limit the company's ability to seek injunctive relief in court, potentially harming its interests. The jurisdiction clause (ISSUE 2) exposes the company to litigation in a jurisdiction where it has no physical presence or connections. Additionally, the arbitration conducted by a sole arbitrator without opt-out for injunctive relief (ISSUE 3) may limit the ability to appeal or challenge the arbitrator's decisions. By replacing the Karnataka State Arbitration Centre with a panel of three arbitrators with expertise in intellectual property and data breach claims, we can improve the fairness and transparency of the arbitration process.
