Our review of the Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) between ABC and XYZ identified a total of 14 clauses, with 4 High Risk Issues and 7 Medium Risk Issues. The top High Risk Issues include:

* Lack of jurisdictional fallback for clients outside India in Clause 01 Governing Law
* Mandatory arbitration without opt-out for injunctive relief in Clause 01 Dispute Resolution

* Lack of notice period for termination and unclear definition of what constitutes the "Entire Agreement" in Clause 01 Entire Agreement

These High Risk Issues have the potential to cause significant financial or legal harm if signed as-is, particularly due to the lack of jurisdictional fallback, mandatory arbitration without opt-out, and unclear intellectual property definitions. The Medium Risk Issues, while not as severe, still warrant attention, including:

* Lack of termination for convenience right in Clause Term and Termination
* Insufficient notice period for termination by either party in Clauses 01 Effective Date and Term and 02 Notice
* Lack of right to terminate for cause with a cure period in Clause 01 Non-Solicitation

Based on our review, we strongly recommend that ABC negotiates the terms of this NDA before signing. The High Risk Issues identified above require careful consideration and potential modifications to ensure that the agreement is fair and balanced for both parties. While some Medium Risk Issues may be addressed through negotiation, it is essential to prioritize addressing the High Risk Issues to avoid potential disputes and financial losses.
