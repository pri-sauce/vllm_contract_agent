## Executive Summary
**Executive Summary**

This Master Service Agreement (MSA) review identified 6 High Risk Issues, 3 Medium Risk Issues, and numerous ACCEPTABLE clauses. The overall contract risk assessment indicates that the MSA contains significant provisions that require attention to mitigate potential financial and legal harm.

The top High Risk Issues include:

* Lack of mutual indemnification for claims arising from both parties' breaches or negligence (PARTIES AND RECITALS)
* Unclear scope definition for IT support services
* Lack of clear payment milestones or schedule
* Insufficient termination notice period for the Service Provider
* Lack of mutual indemnification for third-party claims
* Inadequate carve-out for the Service Provider's background intellectual property

These High Risk Issues may lead to misunderstandings, misinterpretations, and significant financial or legal consequences if left unaddressed. The Medium Risk Issues, while not as severe, still warrant attention to ensure a balanced agreement.

**Recommendation**

Based on this review, we recommend that Company A **Negotiate before signing** the MSA. While some clauses are ACCEPTABLE, the High Risk Issues identified pose significant risks that require careful consideration and negotiation to mitigate. By negotiating these provisions, Company A can ensure a more balanced agreement that protects its interests while maintaining a collaborative relationship with Company B.
