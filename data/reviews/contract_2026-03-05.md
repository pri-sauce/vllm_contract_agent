## Executive Summary

Our review of the Professional Services contract between Company A and Company B identified significant risks and areas for improvement. The contract consists of 27 clauses, with 15 High Risk clauses and 11 Medium Risk clauses. The top High Risk clauses are:

* SERVICE AGREEMENT: One-sided indemnification only against the Service Provider, potentially exposing the Client to unlimited liability for claims arising from the Service Provider's own negligence.
* PARTIES AND RECITALS: One-sided indemnification only against the Service Provider, leaving the Client vulnerable to unlimited liability.
* WHEREAS, the Service Provider is engaged...: Lack of clear definition of data controller vs processor roles, potentially leading to confusion and non-compliance with GDPR and CCPA regulations.

To mitigate these risks, we recommend the following specific changes:

* Replace "the Service Provider shall not be liable for any claims arising" with "the Parties shall indemnify each other against claims arising" in the SERVICE AGREEMENT clause.
* Replace "The Parties agree that the Service Provider shall indemnify " with "The Parties agree that the Service Provider shall indemnify " in the PARTIES AND RECITALS clause.
* Replace "including but not to" with "including but to" in the WHEREAS clause to clarify the data controller vs processor roles.

Based on our review, we recommend that the parties consider the following negotiation priorities:

* Negotiate the indemnification clauses to ensure mutual obligations and clear definitions of roles and responsibilities.
* Negotiate the termination notice period for the Service Provider to ensure a sufficient notice period for both parties.
* Negotiate the compensation and payment terms to include a right to suspend services for non-payment after 30-day notice.

We advise the parties to exercise caution when signing the contract as-is, as the identified risks and areas for improvement may lead to significant financial or legal harm if not addressed. We recommend negotiating the contract terms to ensure a balanced and fair agreement that protects the interests of both parties.
