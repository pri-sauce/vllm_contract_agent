## Executive Summary

Our review of the Master Service Agreement (MSA) between Company A and Company B identified a total of 16 clauses, with 9 High Risk Issues and 2 Medium Risk Issues. The contract's governing law is [State/Country]. Our analysis highlights several critical areas that require attention before signing.

**Critical Issues Requiring Attention**

The top High Risk Issues include one-sided indemnification, lack of clear scope definition for IT support services, lack of clear payment milestones or schedule, perpetual confidentiality obligation, and lack of limitation to claims arising from the indemnifying party's own acts. These clauses could lead to costly disputes, financial harm, and reputational damage if signed as-is. The top Medium Risk Issues are the lack of termination for convenience right with adequate notice period and the lack of carve-out for indemnitee's own negligence in indemnification claims.

**Negotiation Priorities**

To mitigate these risks, we recommend prioritizing negotiations on the following clauses: (1) indemnification provisions to ensure mutual protection against breach or negligence; (2) scope definition for IT support services to avoid misunderstandings; (3) payment milestones and schedule to prevent confusion and disputes; (4) confidentiality obligations to balance business needs with reputational concerns; and (5) termination provisions to provide adequate notice periods. Additionally, we suggest reviewing the representations and warranties section to ensure a fair carve-out for indemnitee's own negligence.

**Recommendation**

Based on our analysis, we recommend that Company A **Negotiate before signing** the MSA. While some clauses are acceptable as-is, the identified High Risk Issues and Medium Risk Issues warrant attention to ensure a balanced and fair agreement. By negotiating these critical provisions, Company A can mitigate potential risks and protect its interests.
