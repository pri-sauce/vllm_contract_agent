EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT

THIS EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT ("Agreement") is made and entered into as of 1st April 2026 (the "Effective Date"), by and between:

ALPHATECH SOLUTIONS PRIVATE LIMITED, a company incorporated under the Companies Act, 2013, having its registered office at [Insert Address], Bangalore, Karnataka, India (hereinafter referred to as the "Company"); AND

MS. PRIYA SHARMA, residing at [Insert Address], Bangalore, Karnataka, India (hereinafter referred to as the "Employee").

WHEREAS, the Company desires to employ the Employee in the capacity of Senior Software Engineer, and the Employee has agreed to serve the Company on the terms and conditions set forth herein;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

---

### 1. APPOINTMENT & POSITION
1.1 The Company hereby appoints the Employee as Senior Software Engineer in the Product Development Department, and the Employee accepts such appointment, effective from the Effective Date.
1.2 The Employee shall report to the Engineering Manager or such other person as the Company may designate from time to time.
1.3 The Employee’s employment shall be full-time and subject to a probation period of six (6) months from the Effective Date, during which either party may terminate this Agreement by providing thirty (30) days’ written notice or payment in lieu thereof. Upon successful completion of the probation period, the Employee shall be confirmed in employment, and the notice period shall extend to sixty (60) days for either party.

---

### 2. DUTIES & WORK ARRANGEMENT
2.1 The Employee shall perform all duties and responsibilities assigned by the Company, including but not limited to software development, system design, code reviews, and team collaboration, in accordance with the Company’s policies and industry best practices.
2.2 The Employee shall work under a hybrid model, requiring physical presence at the Company’s Bangalore office for three (3) days per week and remote work for two (2) days per week, unless otherwise agreed in writing.
2.3 The Employee shall devote their full working time, attention, and skills to the Company’s business and shall not engage in any other employment, business, or professional activity without the Company’s prior written consent.

---

### 3. COMPENSATION & BENEFITS
3.1 The Employee’s annual Cost to Company (CTC) shall be ₹18,00,000 (Eighteen Lakhs Only), inclusive of:
- Basic Salary: As per the Company’s salary structure;
- Allowances: House Rent Allowance (HRA), conveyance, and other allowances as per Company policy;
- Performance Bonus: Up to ₹2,00,000 (Two Lakhs Only), subject to the Company’s performance appraisal process and discretion;
- Statutory Contributions: Provident Fund (PF), Employee State Insurance (ESI), and other statutory deductions as per applicable Indian laws.
3.2 Salary shall be paid monthly via electronic transfer to the Employee’s designated bank account, subject to applicable tax deductions.
3.3 The Company reserves the right to revise the Employee’s compensation, benefits, and role at its sole discretion, with prior written notice.

---

### 4. LEAVE & HOLIDAYS
4.1 The Employee shall be entitled to leave as per the Company’s leave policy and the Shops and Establishments Act, 1961 (Karnataka), including but not limited to:
- Earned Leave (EL): As per Company policy;
- Casual Leave (CL): As per Company policy;
- Sick Leave (SL): As per Company policy and the Employees’ State Insurance Act, 1948 (if applicable);
- Maternity/Paternity Leave: As per the Maternity Benefit Act, 1961 and Company policy;
- Public Holidays: As declared by the Government of Karnataka.
4.2 Unutilized leave shall lapse as per Company policy and shall not be encashable except as provided under applicable law.

---

### 5. CONFIDENTIALITY & INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY (IP)
5.1 The Employee acknowledges that during employment, they may have access to confidential information, including trade secrets, business strategies, client data, and proprietary technology. The Employee agrees to:
- Maintain strict confidentiality during and after employment;
- Use such information solely for the Company’s business purposes;
- Not disclose such information to any third party without prior written consent.
5.2 All inventions, innovations, software, designs, or other intellectual property created by the Employee during employment, whether during working hours or otherwise, shall be the exclusive property of the Company. The Employee hereby assigns all rights, title, and interest in such IP to the Company and agrees to execute any documents necessary to perfect such assignment.
5.3 The obligations under this Clause shall survive the termination of employment indefinitely with respect to trade secrets and for two (2) years for other confidential information.

---

### 6. NON-SOLICITATION & NON-COMPETE
6.1 Non-Solicitation: During employment and for twelve (12) months thereafter, the Employee shall not, directly or indirectly:
- Solicit or induce any Company employee to leave their employment;
- Solicit or divert any Company client, vendor, or business partner for personal gain.
6.2 Non-Compete: For a period of six (6) months post-termination, the Employee shall not, within India, engage in or be associated with any business that competes with the Company’s products, services, or interests, to the extent legally enforceable under Indian law. The Employee acknowledges that this restriction is reasonable and necessary to protect the Company’s legitimate business interests.

---

### 7. TERMINATION
7.1 By the Company: The Company may terminate the Employee’s employment with or without cause by providing sixty (60) days’ written notice (or thirty (30) days’ notice during probation) or payment in lieu thereof. The Company may also terminate employment for cause (e.g., misconduct, breach of this Agreement, fraud) without notice or severance.
7.2 By the Employee: The Employee may resign by providing sixty (60) days’ written notice (or thirty (30) days’ notice during probation).
7.3 Effect of Termination: Upon termination, the Employee shall:
- Return all Company property, documents, and confidential information;
- Cease using the Company’s name, logos, or intellectual property;
- Comply with all post-termination obligations under this Agreement.

---

### 8. DISPUTE RESOLUTION & GOVERNING LAW
8.1 Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall be resolved through arbitration in Bangalore, in accordance with the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996. The arbitration shall be conducted by a sole arbitrator appointed by mutual agreement or, failing such agreement, by the Karnataka State Arbitration Centre.
8.2 The courts in Bangalore shall have exclusive jurisdiction over any matters not subject to arbitration.
8.3 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India.

---

### 9. MISCELLANEOUS
9.1 Entire Agreement: This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the parties and supersedes all prior agreements, representations, or understandings.
9.2 Amendments: Any modifications to this Agreement must be in writing and signed by both parties.
9.3 Severability: If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force.
9.4 Waiver: No waiver of any breach shall constitute a waiver of any subsequent breach.
9.5 Notices: All notices shall be in writing and sent to the addresses specified herein or as subsequently updated by either party.
9.6 Assignment: The Employee may not assign this Agreement without the Company’s prior written consent. The Company may assign this Agreement to any affiliate or successor entity.
9.7 Force Majeure: Neither party shall be liable for failure to perform due to events beyond their reasonable control (e.g., natural disasters, war, government actions).
